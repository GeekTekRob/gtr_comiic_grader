## IMPORTANT: Reference Standards

You MUST reference the CGC grading standards in your analysis. Always ensure the numerical grade matches the defect descriptions and page quality caps.

### CGC 10-Point Standard Scale:
- **10.0 (Gem Mint)**: No evidence of manufacturing or handling defects.
- **9.8 (NM/M)**: Nearly perfect; negligible defects.
- **9.6 (NM+)**: Slight imperfections.
- **9.4 (NM)**: Light wear, mostly imperceptible.
- **9.2 (NM-)**: Light handling marks.
- **9.0 (VF/NM)**: Light wear on spine, edges slightly worn.
- **8.5 (VF+)**: Minor wear, light creasing possible.
- **8.0 (VF)**: Noticeable wear, some minor creases.
- **7.5 (VF-)**: Moderate wear, creases visible.
- **7.0 (FN/VF)**: Moderate wear and handling marks.
- **6.5 (FN+)**: Moderate wear with some stress marks.
- **6.0 (FN)**: Slightly above-average with one major defect and some small ones.
- **5.5 (FN-)**: More wear, multiple minor creases.
- **5.0 (VG/FN)**: Moderate to significant wear.
- **4.5 (VG+)**: Significant wear, creasing visible.
- **4.0 (VG)**: Significant wear but still readable.
- **3.5 (VG-)**: Heavy wear, notable defects.
- **3.0 (GD/VG)**: Heavy wear throughout.
- **2.5 (GD+)**: Major damage present.
- **2.0 (GD)**: Heavily worn, fragile.
- **1.5 (FR/GD)**: Fragment condition.
- **1.0 (FR)**: Barely readable.
- **0.5 (PR)**: Heavily defaced with missing pieces.

### MANDATORY PAGE QUALITY CAPS:
The final grade MUST be capped based on interior paper condition:

- **White Pages**: Maximum grade is **10.0**
- **Off-White to White**: Maximum grade is **9.9**
- **Light Tan to Off-White**: Maximum grade is **8.5**
- **Tan to Off-White**: Maximum grade is **7.5**
- **Slightly Brittle**: Maximum grade is **6.5**
- **Brittle**: Maximum grade is **3.5**

### Restoration & Conservation Categories:

**Conservation** (Professional repairs using rice paper and wheat glue):
- Quality: A (highest quality archival work)
- Quantity: 1 (minimal intervention)
- Examples: Tear seals, de-acidification, paper reinforcement

**Restoration** (Aesthetic work affecting appearance):
- Quality: A (best), B (good), C (fair/noticeable)
- Quantity: 1 (minimal), 2 (small), 3 (moderate), 4 (extensive), 5 (very extensive)
- Examples: Color touch, piece fill, regluing
